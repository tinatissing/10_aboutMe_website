Speed Stacking

What is Speed Stacking?

Speed stacking also known as sport stacking or cup stacking is a sport where you stack plastic cups up and down as fast as you can in a certain succession. The three official patterns are in three pyramids of three, 3-3-3, two pyramids of three and one pyramid of six, 3-6-3, and a cycle stack that includes all different kinds of pyramids. You can stack as an individual, a pair (doubles), or a team of four (relay). This is an official sport in the Junior Olympics, and Canada hosted the World Championships for the first time in Montreal, 2015.

Different Ways to Stack

There are many different patterns in which you can stack cups. In competitions they will only time you in three individual events, the 3-3-3, the 3-6-3, and the Cycle, which is the most popular if the three. Once you hit the official competition table, you get two practices and three real recorded tries at each pattern of stacking.

3-3-3

The 3-3-3 stack is the simplest of the three. You stack three small pyramids of three cups in order, then stack them down in the same order you up stacked them. The world record for this is 1.363 seconds, held by William Orrell (team USA).

3-6-3

The 3-6-3 is a little more complicated than the 3-3-3 stack. You stack one pyramid of three, one pyramid of six cups, and another pyramid of three. Like the 3-3-3, you have to stack them all down after in the same order you stacked them up. The world record for this stack is 1.793, also held by William Orrell.

Cycle

The cycle is the most popular pattern. It's much more complicated then either of the other stacks. The world record holder of this stack is once again, William Orrell.
